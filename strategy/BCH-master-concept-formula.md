# PHASE 2: HOOK ENGINEERING â€” FIRST 3 SECONDS

## 2.1 The 12-Point Hook Scoring Framework

**Score EVERY concept on these 12 dimensions BEFORE shooting. Minimum composite: 7.5/10.**

| # | Dimension | What It Measures | Score 8+ Looks Like | Score Below 6 |
|---|-----------|-----------------|--------------------|--------------|
| 1 | **Involuntary Attention Capture** | Does frame 1 stop scrolling automatically? | Fire, money, danger, faces, anomaly, destruction | Man standing next to bike |
| 2 | **Cognitive Disruption** | Does the visual violate expectations? | Funeral for a cycle, drinking petrol, CCTV of a kid buying | Standard showroom demo |
| 3 | **Curiosity Gap** | Is there an unanswered question? | "Why is he burning a cycle?" "Why did he faint?" | No question â€” viewer knows what happens next |
| 4 | **Emotional Micro-Trigger** | Does it fire an emotion in <1 second? | Shock, greed, parent guilt, laughter, pride | Purely informational, no feeling |
| 5 | **Platform-Native Twist** | Does it feel like a Reel, not a YouTube ad? | Street Kannada, handheld, UGC energy, trending format | Studio-shot, corporate, landscape video |
| 6 | **Sound-Off Effectiveness** | Does it work fully on mute? | Text overlay carries full hook; visual tells story alone | 100% audio-dependent; mute = meaningless |
| 7 | **Relevance Bridge** | How fast does viewer connect hook to product? | By 0:07 maximum | Product appears only at 0:20+ with no bridge |
| 8 | **Motion/Kinetics** | Is there visual movement in first 3 sec? | 3+ distinct motions, gimbal, cuts, body movement | Static tripod, no zoom, person standing still |
| 9 | **Contrast/Color** | Does the visual pop against a feed? | High-contrast text, bright clothing, product color pop | Dull, low-contrast, blends into feed |
| 10 | **Repetition Resilience** | Does it stay engaging on rewatch? | Emotional depth, new details on repeat | One-time gag, novelty gone after first view |
| 11 | **Thumbnail-Hook Alignment** | Does thumbnail = first frame? | Exact match, zero bait-and-switch | Different thumbnail, viewer clicks and leaves |
| 12 | **Shareability Signal** | Does the hook trigger sharing urge? | "I NEED to send this to someone" within 3 sec | Nothing worth forwarding |

**Composite Score â†’ Expected Performance:**

## 2.2 The 4 Hook Layers

**Design ALL 4 for every concept. All 4 must deliver the SAME message.**

| Layer | What It Is | Rule | Example (V9 â€” Burning Cycle, scored 9.0) |
|-------|-----------|------|-------------------------------------------|
| **Visual** | Physical action that stops the scroll | Must be dramatic, visceral, physical â€” NOT a phone screen, text chat, or subtle expression | Man LITERALLY sets old cycle on FIRE |
| **Text** | On-screen overlay creating curiosity | Appears by 0:02. 1-3 lines max. High-contrast color. | "King of Cycles / Exchange Offer" |
| **Spoken** | Bold opening line in Kannada | Fires simultaneously with visual, not after. Max 2-3 sentences. | "Old cycle iddre fire hakbidi!" |
| **SFX** | Sound/editing punch that amplifies | Must amplify the visual, not compete with it | Fire crackling + dramatic bass drop |

**Critical Rule:** If visual says "fire," text says "exchange offer," and spoken says "come to our store" â€” the hook is MISALIGNED and will underperform. All 4 layers must point to the same message.

# PHASE 3: STORY ARCHITECTURE

## 3.1 Story Shape Selection

| Shape | Pattern | Best For | Watch-Through | Risk |
|-------|---------|----------|---------------|------|
| **Ascending Staircase** | Each beat higher than previous, pure climb | Kid achievement, family transformation | Highest (53%+) | No contrast moment |
| **J-Curve** | Dip â†’ Rise â†’ Peak | Mother dragging kid by ear â†’ reward | 50-70% | Needs strong dip |
| **PAS Comedy Pivot** | Problem â†’ Agitation â†’ Solution | Comedy-to-commerce, pain point â†’ product | 81.5% (V80 â€” highest) | Must commit to comedy first |
| **Dual-Peak** | Peak 1 (hook) â†’ sustain â†’ Peak 2 (reveal) | Cultural reference + feature cascade | 60-67% | Middle must not sag |
| **Ramp-Up + Plateau** | Hook spike â†’ sustained interest â†’ desire plateau | Found money, street skit â†’ product | 70%+ view-past-3s | Lacks dramatic valley |
| **U-Curve / Bookend** | Open peak + close peak, valley in middle | Extended kid buyer journey | 29-32% (LOW) | Middle section kills retention |
| **Front-Loaded Spike** | Peak at 0:00-0:07, gradual decline | Price-only hooks, bait format | 32% (LOW) | All energy spent too early |

**Best performers by watch-through:** PAS Comedy Pivot (81.5%) > Ramp-Up (70%+) > J-Curve (50-70%) > Staircase (53%+)

## 3.2 Beat Structures

| Structure | Total Duration | Beats | Best For |
|-----------|---------------|-------|----------|
| **3-Beat** | 15-25 sec | Hook (3-5s) â†’ Proof (5-10s) â†’ CTA (3-5s) | Price hooks, bait-and-switch |
| **4-Beat** | 25-35 sec | Hook (3-5s) â†’ Build (5-10s) â†’ Peak (3-7s) â†’ CTA (3-5s) | Balanced story + resolution |
| **5-Beat** | 30-45 sec | Cultural Hook â†’ Product Intro â†’ Feature Demo â†’ Wow Reveal â†’ CTA | Cultural reference + cascade |
| **6-Beat** | 45-60 sec | Hook â†’ Build â†’ Explore â†’ Climax â†’ Resolve â†’ CTA | Customer transformation |
| **7-Beat** | 60-90 sec | Hook â†’ Arrival â†’ Connection â†’ Showcase â†’ Climax â†’ Celebration â†’ CTA | Full family narrative |

**Rules:**
- Average shot duration: 2.5-4 seconds (not over 5 except for emotional moments)
- 15-20 cuts minimum in feature sections
- Each beat should escalate energy or reveal new information
- Never drop more than 2 energy points between consecutive beats

## 3.3 Twist Timing

| Placement | When | Use For | Risk |
|-----------|------|---------|------|
| **Early (5-15% of video)** | 0:03-0:07 | Bait-and-switch, instant proof | Peaks too early, rest declines |
| **Mid (40-70% of video)** | 0:20-0:40 | Standard optimal for retention | Requires buildup |
| **Late (75-90% of video)** | 0:45-0:55 | Maximum watch-time banking | Most viewers drop before this |

**Optimal:** Twist at 65% mark for 60-90 second content. Setup-to-twist gap: 10-20 seconds.

**Twist velocity rule:** Claim-to-proof within 5 seconds. "This folds like paper" (0:00) â†’ actual fold proof (0:05) = maximum impact.

## 3.4 Unreliable Opening Techniques

Every video scoring 8+ uses one of these:

| Type | Technique | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Genre Misdirection** | Hook looks like one content type, reveals as another | V80: Looks like comedy skit â†’ actually cycle shop |
| **Expectation Reversal** | Opens suggesting one outcome, delivers opposite | V2: Mother drags kid by ear â†’ actually giving a gift |
| **Numerical Misdirection** | Price/number misleads initially | V26: Rs.999 sounds like outright price â†’ is monthly EMI |
| **Skeptic Character** | Opens with doubt/skepticism, character converts | V17: "Is this electrical?" â†’ convinced by features |
| **Visual Contradiction** | What you see contradicts what you hear/expect | V80: Man drinking petrol = absurd visual, real pain point |

## 3.5 Saggy Middle Prevention

**The middle section (0:15-0:35) is where 40-60% of viewers drop. Prevent it with:**

1. **Sub-beat every 3-5 seconds** â€” one new value/info per sub-beat
2. **Intercut with reaction clips** â€” showroom (4s) â†” reaction (2s) pattern
3. **Visual variety within same location** â€” close-ups rotating: pedals, handlebar, display, battery, frame
4. **Music beat-matching** â€” features sync to audio hits for rhythmic pacing
5. **Feature escalation** â€” each feature bigger/better than last (not same energy level)
6. **Thread a narrative through features** â€” "Rocky Bhai would ride THIS because..." for every feature

**What causes sag:** Same energy start to finish, 25+ second product parade, no escalation, same camera angle for 15+ seconds.

# PHASE 7: KILL TESTS â€” FINAL GATE

**Every concept must pass ALL 6 tests before shooting. Fail any = rework.**

## Test 1: Share Test
> "Would someone screenshot this and send it to a SPECIFIC person?"

If no clear recipient relationship exists (parent-to-parent, friend-to-commuter, spouse-to-spouse), the concept lacks share motivation.

## Test 2: Comment Test
> "Is there a built-in reason for viewers to comment?"

Best triggers: "Drop your [X] below," "Tag someone who [Y]," polarizing question, debate angle.

## Test 3: Memory Test
> "Describe this concept to someone a week later. Do they remember it?"

The concept must have ONE vivid image that sticks: "That reel where the guy burned his cycle," "That reel where the kid fainted at the price."

## Test 4: Chai Test
> "Could you describe this to a friend over chai in one sentence?"

If it takes more than one sentence, the concept is too complex. The single sentence IS the concept. If it doesn't make them react, it's not a concept.

## Test 5: TG Filter Test
> "If this goes viral, will the viewers actually be potential BUYERS?"

Show the concept to 3 people NOT in your TG. If they love it too, the hook is too generic (Tata Ace problem).

## Test 6: Tata Ace Test
> "Strong hook + wrong audience = wasted content."

The hook must attract the RIGHT person (parent, commuter, cyclist), not just ANY person. A burning cycle attracts destruction-lovers AND exchange-seekers. A kid fainting at price attracts only price-conscious parents. The second is better targeted.

# PHASE 8: DIRECTION NOTES TEMPLATE

**Include these with EVERY concept:**

## 8.1 Videographer Rules (From High-Performers)
- Camera angles: Low ground-level, over-shoulder, progressive zoom-in, POV from vehicle
- Natural light preferred over studio (outdoor/golden hour)
- Handheld for first 3 seconds (authenticity), gimbal for body if needed
- Collect 2-3 camera angles per scene for editorial flexibility
- Every movement serves the narrative â€” no movement for movement's sake
- Close-ups: Rotate subjects every 2-3 seconds (pedals, handlebar, display, battery, seat)

## 8.2 Editor Rules (From High-Performers)
- Cut timing: 2.5-3.5 second average shot in hook
- Text animation: Different effect per tier (fire = price, glow = features, 3D = product name)
- Beat matching: Audio + cut + text all land simultaneously
- Sound-off strategy: From 0:00-0:03, assume 50-70% are on mute. Hook must work visually.
- Energy curve engineering: Start HIGH, dip LOW mid-section, spike HIGH at CTA
- No English subtitles needed for Karnataka audience â€” Kannada audio + English text overlay sufficient

## 8.3 Talent Direction Rules
- **3x exaggeration rule:** What looks "too much" on set looks "just right" on phone
- **Staff tone:** Casual, bored-confident, like this happens 10 times a day (contrast between calm staff and shocked customer = comedy)
- **Customer tone:** Genuine reactions only. Never say "act surprised" â€” create actual surprise.
- **Kids:** ZERO scripting. Let them speak naturally. Scripted kid = instant audience detection = video fails.
- **Facial expressions:** Wide eyes, jaw drop, physical flinch â€” visible on a 6-inch screen
- **Body contact with product:** SLAP, BANG, THROW items. Physical contact = subconscious ownership transfer for viewer.
- **No background music for conversion reels.** Kannada vocal energy carries retention. Reserve music for entertainment/celebration content.
