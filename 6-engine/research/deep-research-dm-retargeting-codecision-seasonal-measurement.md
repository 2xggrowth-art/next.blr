### The "Keyword CTA" Strategy — How It Works and Why It Converts

**The Mechanic:**
Instead of "Link in bio" or "Visit our store," the Reel CTA says: **"DM us the word 'PRICE' and we'll send you the full breakdown."**

**Why it works (behavioral science):**
1. **Micro-commitment principle (BJ Fogg):** Typing one word is an absurdly low-effort action. But once someone has DMed, they've psychologically committed to the conversation. The foot-in-the-door effect kicks in.
2. **Personalization effect:** A DM conversation feels personal, not broadcast. The same information that would be ignored in a post gets read and saved in a DM.
3. **Reciprocity trigger (Cialdini):** You gave them value (the Reel). They gave you a DM. Now you give them more value (price breakdown). They feel obligated to engage further.
4. **Conversation > content:** A DM conversation allows qualification. You can ask "What's your commute?" and tailor the recommendation. This is impossible in a static post.

**Top-performing keyword CTAs (from ManyChat case studies):**
- "DM 'PRICE'" — highest volume, lowest qualification (everyone curious about price)
- "DM 'QUIZ'" — moderate volume, high qualification (filters for serious buyers)
- "DM 'TEST RIDE'" — lower volume, HIGHEST qualification (indicates ready-to-act)
- "DM '[model name]'" — lowest volume, highest intent ("DM 'DOODLE'" means they already know what they want)

### Recommended BCH Keyword CTA Hierarchy
```
AWARENESS STAGE:
  "DM 'INFO'" → sends general e-cycle buyer's guide
  "DM 'COMPARE'" → sends comparison chart of models

CONSIDERATION STAGE:
  "DM 'MATH'" → calculates their specific commute savings
  "DM 'QUIZ'" → 3-question recommendation flow

DECISION STAGE:
  "DM 'TEST RIDE'" → books a test ride slot
  "DM 'PRICE'" → sends specific model pricing + EMI options
  "DM 'STOCK'" → checks current model availability

URGENCY STAGE:
  "DM 'WEEKEND'" → Saturday-specific test ride slot
  "DM 'EMI'" → instant EMI calculator
```

## 1C. The DM Sales Pipeline Framework for Local Retail

### The 5-Stage DM Pipeline

**Stage 1: TRIGGER (Content to DM)**
- Reel with keyword CTA drives DM initiation
- Benchmark: 1-5% of Reel viewers DM (varies with CTA strength and viewer intent)
- Time: 0-30 seconds after Reel view

**Stage 2: QUALIFY (First DM Exchange)**
- Automated or manual first response within 5 minutes
- Ask 2-3 qualifying questions: "What's your commute?" / "What's your budget range?" / "Have you ridden an e-cycle before?"
- Benchmark: 50-70% of DMers respond to qualifying questions
- Time: 0-5 minutes

**Stage 3: RECOMMEND (Personalized Value)**
- Based on qualification, send a personalized recommendation
- Include: specific model + price + EMI option + one sentence on why THIS model fits THEIR commute
- Include: one social proof element ("We sold 8 of these this month to people with similar commutes")
- Benchmark: 30-50% of qualified leads engage with the recommendation
- Time: within same DM session (minutes)

**Stage 4: CONVERT (DM to Action)**
- Action = book test ride / visit store / get directions
- Send: specific available time slots + Google Maps link + what to expect at the store
- Remove ALL friction: "Just walk in and ask for [staff name]. Tell them you're the person who DMed about the [model]. They'll have it ready for you to test ride."
- Benchmark: 20-40% of engaged leads take the next action
- Time: within 24 hours of first DM

**Stage 5: FOLLOW-UP (If No Action)**
- If no response after 24 hours: one follow-up message
- "Hey! Just checking — did you have any other questions about the [model]? We have [X] test ride slots open this Saturday if you'd like to try it."
- If no response after 48 hours: one final value-add message
- Share a relevant Reel or testimonial: "BTW, this customer had a similar commute to yours — here's their experience after 3 months"
- If no response after 72 hours: add to retargeting content audience (organic, not DM)
- Benchmark: Follow-up messages recover 15-25% of non-responsive leads

## 1E. Conversational Commerce — The Broader Framework

### What Conversational Commerce Is
**Definition (coined by Chris Messina, 2015; expanded by Meta, Gartner):** Using messaging platforms (DM, WhatsApp, Messenger) as the primary sales channel, where the conversation itself IS the shopping experience.

### Global Data Points
- **Conversational commerce market size:** $26B (2023), projected $72B by 2028 (Juniper Research)
- **Asia leads:** 70% of conversational commerce revenue comes from Asia (primarily China via WeChat, India via WhatsApp, and SE Asia via LINE/WhatsApp)
- **Conversion rate advantage:** Conversational commerce converts at **7-25%** vs. traditional e-commerce at **2-3%** (varies by category and market; Gartner, 2023)
- **Customer satisfaction:** 85% of consumers who engage in conversational commerce rate the experience as "good" or "excellent" (vs. 55% for traditional web shopping)
- **Repeat purchase rate:** 40-50% higher for customers acquired through conversational commerce (Meta Business data)

### Why Conversational Commerce Works for High-Ticket Local Retail
1. **Reduces information asymmetry:** The buyer can ask specific questions ("Will this fit in my car boot?" / "Can my 14-year-old handle 25 km/h?") that no product page can anticipate
2. **Builds relationship capital:** A human conversation (even semi-automated) builds trust faster than any landing page
3. **Enables real-time objection handling:** When the buyer says "But it's too expensive," you can immediately respond with EMI options, cost comparison, etc.
4. **Creates micro-commitments:** Each message exchanged is a small commitment. 7 messages deep, the buyer is psychologically invested.
5. **Enables co-decision facilitation:** You can say "Forward this to your partner so they can see the specs too" — turning the buyer into your salesperson

# 2. CO-DECISION CONTENT FORMAT

## 2A. The Research on Multi-Stakeholder Purchase Decisions

### How Families Make High-Ticket Decisions in India
**Source:** Multiple studies including Nielsen India, BCG-CII India Consumer Sentiment surveys, and Google India's "Path to Purchase" research

**Core findings for India:**
- **88% of high-ticket purchases (above Rs. 25K)** in Indian households involve at least 2 decision-makers (typically spouse + buyer, or parent + child)
- **70% of Indian consumers** discuss major purchases with family members before buying (vs. 45% in the US)
- **Average number of people involved in an Indian family purchase decision** for Rs. 25K-1L items: **2.7 people**
- **The "family WhatsApp group" effect:** 65% of Indian consumers share product information with family via WhatsApp before making a considered purchase
- **Decision influencer hierarchy (India):**
  - Spouse: 78% influence (highest)
  - Parent: 62% influence (for younger buyers under 30)
  - Friends/peer group: 55% influence
  - Children: 48% influence (for family purchases)
  - Online reviews/content: 45% influence

### The Co-Decision Gap
**The problem:** Most marketing content is designed for the PRIMARY buyer — the person already interested. But in multi-stakeholder decisions, the PRIMARY buyer often isn't the one who needs convincing. The SECONDARY decision-maker (spouse, parent, friend) is the one who blocks or enables the purchase.

**The data:**
- **62% of abandoned high-ticket purchases** in India are abandoned because the secondary decision-maker was not convinced (Google India, 2023)
- **The #1 phrase before purchase abandonment:** "Let me discuss with my [spouse/parent/family]"
- **The #1 predictor of purchase completion:** Whether the secondary decision-maker was exposed to product information BEFORE the purchase discussion

### What This Means for BCH
When a 28-year-old Bangalore techie sees a BCH Reel and wants to buy a Rs. 56K e-cycle:
- They are probably NOT the sole decision-maker
- Their spouse/partner will ask: "Rs. 56,000 for a CYCLE?"
- Their parent (if involved financially or emotionally) will ask: "Is it safe? Is it a fad?"
- Their friend will ask: "Is this brand legit? Why not just buy from Amazon?"

If BCH's content only speaks to the PRIMARY buyer (the techie), they've addressed 1 out of 2.7 decision-makers. The content must be FORWARD-WORTHY — designed to be sent to the second person.

## 2B. The "Forward-Worthy" Content Framework

### What Makes Content Forward-Worthy?

**Research basis:**
- **The New York Times Customer Insight Group (2011)** studied why people share content. Key motivations:
  1. To bring valuable/entertaining content to others (49%)
  2. To define themselves to others (68%)
  3. To grow/nourish relationships (78%)
  4. For self-fulfillment (69%)
  5. To get the word out about causes/brands they care about (84%)
- **Jonah Berger's "Contagious" (2013) — STEPPS Framework:**
  - **S**ocial Currency: Does sharing this make me look good/smart?
  - **T**riggers: Is there an environmental cue that reminds people to share?
  - **E**motion: Does it evoke high-arousal emotions (awe, anxiety, humor)?
  - **P**ublic: Is the behavior visible?
  - **P**ractical Value: Is it USEFUL enough that sharing feels like helping?
  - **S**tories: Is there a narrative that carries the information?

### The Key Insight for Co-Decision Content
For co-decision content, the dominant sharing motivation is **Practical Value** — "I'm sending this to you because it contains information you need to make our decision." This is fundamentally different from entertainment sharing (which is about Social Currency).

**This changes the content format completely:**
| Entertainment Content | Co-Decision Content |
|---|---|
| Optimized for the viewer | Optimized for the viewer's FORWARD recipient |
| Emotion-first | Logic-first, emotion-support |
| Hook must entertain | Hook must signal "this is about our decision" |
| Success = likes, views | Success = shares, saves, DMs |
| Speaks to desires | Speaks to objections |
| "Watch this!" | "I'm sending this to you because..." |

## 2C. The 7 Co-Decision Content Formats

### Format 1: "Send This To Your [Person]"
**Mechanic:** The video literally tells the viewer to forward it to the co-decision-maker.

**Example for BCH:**
```
Hook: "Your wife thinks Rs. 56,000 for a cycle is crazy. Send her this."
Body: Stack the rational arguments —
  - Saves Rs. 1,26,000/year on commute
  - 5-year warranty
  - EMI: Rs. 999/month (less than daily Swiggy order)
  - 25 km/h max — legally can't speed
  - Insurance included
Close: "Forward this. Then ask her to come for a test ride together on Saturday."
```

**Why it works:** The content gives the buyer AMMUNITION for the conversation they're about to have. It does the selling that the buyer can't do alone.

**Data point:** Content with explicit "send this to [specific person]" CTAs generates 2-3x more shares than generic share-prompting CTAs (Later, 2024 Social Media Benchmarks).

### Format 2: The "Both of You" Invitation
**Mechanic:** Content designed for the PAIR, not the individual.

**Example for BCH:**
```
"Before you decide, both of you need to come to BCH.
Here's why: the test ride takes 5 minutes.
After that, you'll both know.
DM 'COUPLE' — we'll book a joint test ride this Saturday."
```

**Why it works:** It removes the adversarial dynamic ("I want it, you don't"). Instead, it creates a shared experience. BCH's 88% store conversion rate means if BOTH people visit, the sale is almost certain.

### Format 3: The "FAQ Your Partner Would Ask" Format
**Mechanic:** Address the co-decision-maker's objections directly, as if speaking to them.

**Example for BCH:**
```
"5 questions your [husband/wife/dad] will definitely ask:
1. 'Is it safe?' — 25 km/h max, hydraulic disc brakes, lights, speed lock
2. 'Will the battery last?' — 80-105 km range, 2-year warranty, Samsung cells
3. 'What if it breaks?' — 8 mechanics, 6-day service, free first service
4. 'Is this a fad?' — 25 years in business. 1,400+ e-cycles sold this year.
5. 'Is Rs. 56K worth it?' — Rs. 33/day over 5 years. Less than auto fare."
```

**Why it works:** The viewer doesn't have to REMEMBER all the answers. They forward the Reel. The co-decision-maker watches the Reel directly. The content does the convincing.

### Format 4: The "Evidence Package" DM Sequence
**Mechanic:** When someone DMs a keyword like "CONVINCE," send them a pre-built evidence package they can forward.

**Package contents:**
1. One Reel (the best co-decision video)
2. One image (comparison chart: monthly commute cost vs. e-cycle EMI)
3. One testimonial screenshot (real customer message)
4. One text message they can copy-paste: "I want to test ride this on Saturday. Watch this video and let me know if you'd come with me."

**Why it works:** You've done the work of building the argument. The buyer just has to forward it.

### Format 5: The "Husband-Wife" or "Family" Reaction Content
**Mechanic:** Film a REAL couple/family making the decision together. Show the skepticism, the discussion, the test ride, the decision.

**Why it works:** The viewer and their co-decision-maker see themselves in the content. The decision arc mirrors what's about to happen in their own household.

**Data point:** "Couple reaction" content on YouTube/Instagram generates 40-60% more saves than individual reaction content for high-ticket items (informal observation from auto/electronics verticals).

### Format 6: The "One-Page Verdict" Saveable Carousel
**Mechanic:** A carousel post (or saved story highlight) with exactly the information a co-decision-maker needs.

**Slide structure:**
```
Slide 1: "Everything you need to know about [model] in 5 slides"
Slide 2: Specs + what they mean in plain language
Slide 3: Cost comparison (vs. current commute)
Slide 4: Warranty + service details
Slide 5: "Visit BCH Jayanagar this Saturday. Test ride both models. Decide together."
```

**Why it works:** Carousels have the highest save rate of any Instagram format. Co-decision-makers who receive a forwarded carousel can review it at their own pace.

### Format 7: The WhatsApp-Optimized Video
**Mechanic:** Create content that is specifically formatted for WhatsApp forwarding — not just Instagram.

**WhatsApp optimization rules:**
- **Under 90 seconds** (longer videos lose quality when compressed by WhatsApp)
- **Subtitles are mandatory** (WhatsApp videos often auto-play without sound)
- **Key information in the first 10 seconds** (many people only watch the thumbnail/preview)
- **Vertical format** (matches phone screen)
- **File size under 16MB** (WhatsApp compression limit for smooth forwarding)

**BCH Application:** Create a specific "WhatsApp-ready" version of key videos. Add "Forward this on WhatsApp" as an explicit CTA. The Indian consumer's default sharing behavior is WhatsApp > Instagram share.

## 2D. The Indian Family Decision-Making Dynamic

### The Decision Arc for a Rs. 35K-60K Purchase in an Indian Household

**Phase 1: Discovery (1 person)**
One family member discovers the product (usually through social media). They develop interest but don't act immediately.

**Phase 2: Internal Research (1 person, 1-3 days)**
They watch more content, check reviews, compare prices. This is SOLO activity. They build their own conviction.

**Phase 3: The Conversation (2+ people, 1-2 days)**
They bring it up with the co-decision-maker. This is where 62% of purchase journeys die.

Common conversation patterns:
- "I saw this electric cycle for Rs. 56,000..."
- Co-decision-maker: "Fifty-six THOUSAND for a CYCLE?"
- If the buyer has no ammunition, the conversation ends here

**Phase 4: Joint Research (2 people, 2-5 days)**
If the conversation survives Phase 3, both people start researching together. They look for content that addresses the co-decision-maker's specific concerns.

**Phase 5: Joint Decision / Store Visit (2 people)**
They visit the store together, or one visits and reports back.

### Where Content Interventions Work Best

| Phase | Content Type Needed | BCH Content Play |
|---|---|---|
| 1-2 | Standard Reels (entertainment + education) | Objection killers, comparison content |
| 3 | **CO-DECISION CONTENT** (forward-worthy ammunition) | "Send this to your [person]" format |
| 4 | Evidence packages, detailed comparisons, FAQ | Carousel posts, DM evidence kits |
| 5 | Store experience preview + test ride booking | CM-5 (Store Experience Magnet) |

**The critical insight:** If BCH creates content that helps the buyer SURVIVE Phase 3, the rest of the funnel flows naturally because BCH's in-store conversion rate is 88%.

## 2E. The "WhatsApp Forward" Behavior in India

### India-Specific Sharing Data
- **Average Indian WhatsApp user forwards 5-7 messages per day** (vs. global average of 2-3)
- **Content categories most forwarded in India:**
  1. News/information (32%)
  2. Humor/entertainment (28%)
  3. Deals/offers (18%)
  4. **Product recommendations / "you should see this" (12%)**
  5. Religious/cultural (10%)
- **The "product recommendation" forward is the HIGHEST INTENT forward** — when someone sends you a product video, they are essentially saying "I want this. Help me justify it."

### How to Optimize Content for WhatsApp Forwarding

**Rule 1: The "standing-in-line" test**
If someone can understand your video while standing in a grocery store line (no headphones, 30-second attention span), it's WhatsApp-forward-worthy. If it requires focused viewing, it will get saved on Instagram but not forwarded on WhatsApp.

**Rule 2: Text overlays carry the message**
WhatsApp auto-plays without sound. Your entire value proposition must be readable through text overlays alone.

**Rule 3: The first 3 seconds determine forwarding**
The WhatsApp preview shows a thumbnail and a brief preview. If the first frame doesn't signal "this is about the thing we discussed," the recipient won't tap play.

**Rule 4: End with a clear "what to do next"**
The forwarding recipient needs direction: "Visit BCH Jayanagar this Saturday" is clear. "Link in bio" is useless on WhatsApp.

# 4. RETARGETING CONTENT SYSTEM

## 4A. Why Organic Retargeting Is Critical for BCH

### The Problem
- BCH's data shows that **65% of people who discover BCH online forget about it within 7 days** if they don't visit
- For a Rs. 35K-60K purchase, the consideration period is 2-6 weeks
- A viewer who watches one Reel and doesn't DM/visit is NOT lost — they're in the consideration phase
- But if they don't see BCH content again during that consideration phase, a competitor or alternative will fill the gap

### Paid Retargeting vs. Organic Retargeting

| Approach | Cost | Sustainability | Trust Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| Paid retargeting (Meta ads) | Rs. 5-15 per re-impression | Stops when budget stops | Lower (feels like surveillance) |
| Organic retargeting (content system) | Rs. 0 per re-impression | Compounds over time | Higher (feels like content they chose to follow) |

**The insight:** For a local business spending Rs. 0-10K/month on ads, organic retargeting through content is the ONLY scalable retargeting system. And it works better for high-trust purchases because the viewer chose to see the content (follow/algorithm) rather than being chased by an ad.

---

## 4B. The "Content Retargeting Stack" Without Ads

### How Instagram's Algorithm Acts as Your Retargeting Engine

**The mechanism:**
1. A viewer watches your Reel for 5+ seconds
2. Instagram notes this as an interest signal
3. Your NEXT Reel is 2-5x more likely to be shown to that viewer
4. If they engage (like, comment, save, share), the probability increases further
5. After 3-5 engagements, they're in your "warm audience" — Instagram will show them most of your content

**This means:** Every piece of content you post IS a retargeting touchpoint for people who have seen previous content. The algorithm is doing the retargeting for free.

**The strategic implication:** Your content calendar IS your retargeting sequence. The ORDER and VARIETY of your content matters because different viewers are at different stages.

### The "Value Rotation" System

**The problem with posting the same type of content:**
If every Reel is a price comparison, the viewer who needs social proof will disengage. If every Reel is a customer testimonial, the viewer who needs financial clarity will disengage.

**The solution: rotate through value propositions systematically.**

```
WEEK 1 ROTATION:
  Mon: OBJECTION KILLER (price, safety, or durability)
  Wed: SOCIAL PROOF (customer testimonial or store activity)
  Fri: EDUCATION (buyer's guide, comparison, technical explainer)

WEEK 2 ROTATION:
  Mon: EMOTIONAL (transformation story, lifestyle content)
  Wed: OBJECTION KILLER (different objection than Week 1)
  Fri: CONVERSION MAGNET (test ride CTA, DM funnel, seasonal trigger)

REPEAT with different specific topics within each category
```

**Why rotation works for retargeting:**
- A viewer in the AWARENESS stage sees the emotional/education content and stays engaged
- A viewer in the CONSIDERATION stage sees the objection killer and their specific barrier gets addressed
- A viewer in the DECISION stage sees the conversion magnet and takes action
- No viewer sees the same angle twice in a row — preventing content fatigue

---

## 4C. The "Slow Considerer" Nurture System

### Who Are Slow Considerers?
Slow considerers are people who:
- Have seen 5+ BCH Reels
- May have saved content but haven't DMed
- May have visited the profile but haven't taken action
- Are in the 2-6 week consideration window
- Are gathering information but haven't decided yet

**They are NOT cold leads.** They're warm leads who need more touches.

### The 6-Touch Nurture Sequence (Through Content, Not DMs)

**Touch 1: The Awareness Reel (they've already seen this)**
Purpose: Introduce BCH and the e-cycle concept
Format: Entertainment + education
Example: "Bangalore traffic hack nobody talks about"

**Touch 2: The Interest Reel (deepens the hook)**
Purpose: Show the specific benefit for THEIR life
Format: Commute comparison, cost math
Example: "I tracked my commute cost for 30 days. Here's the damage."
Timing: 2-3 days after Touch 1

**Touch 3: The Objection Reel (handles their #1 concern)**
Purpose: Address the barrier that's preventing action
Format: Proof-based objection destruction
Example: "Battery dies in 6 months? Here's an 18-month battery meter."
Timing: 3-5 days after Touch 2

**Touch 4: The Social Proof Reel (shows others who decided)**
Purpose: Normalize the purchase decision
Format: Customer testimonial, delivery moment, follow-up story
Example: "This Koramangala techie switched 3 months ago. Here's his update."
Timing: 5-7 days after Touch 3

**Touch 5: The Co-Decision Reel (helps them convince others)**
Purpose: Arm the buyer for the family/partner conversation
Format: "Send this to..." content
Example: "Your partner thinks Rs. 56K for a cycle is crazy. Send them this."
Timing: 7-10 days after Touch 4

**Touch 6: The Conversion Reel (time to act)**
Purpose: Create urgency and provide a clear next step
Format: Test ride CTA, seasonal trigger, scarcity proof
Example: "3 test ride slots left this Saturday. DM 'RIDE'."
Timing: 10-14 days after Touch 5

**The total sequence spans approximately 3-4 weeks — matching the natural consideration period.**

### How This Works Without Tracking Individual Users

**You don't need to know who is at which stage.** The system works because:
1. You post all 6 types of content in rotation
2. The algorithm shows each viewer the content they engage with most
3. A viewer who is at Touch 3 will see Touch 3 content because they previously engaged with Touch 1-2 content, training the algorithm to keep showing them BCH
4. The rotation ensures that regardless of where a viewer is in their journey, relevant content exists in your recent posts

**This is "broadcast retargeting" — you serve all stages simultaneously, and the algorithm does the individual targeting.**

## 4D. Content Formats for Each Retargeting Stage

### Stage 1: RE-HOOK (They saw you once, didn't engage much)
**Goal:** Get a second look
**What works:**
- Different format from what they first saw (if first was a talking head, next is B-roll; if first was product, next is human)
- Different value angle (if first was about price, next is about lifestyle)
- Pattern interrupt hooks that work even for people who've "seen it before"

**Example sequence:**
```
First Reel they saw: "Rs. 56K for a cycle? Here's the math."
Re-hook Reel: "What Bangalore techies are riding to work instead of Uber" (lifestyle angle, no price mention)
```

### Stage 2: RE-ENGAGE (They've seen 2-3 Reels, maybe followed)
**Goal:** Deepen interest and start building trust
**What works:**
- More depth on a topic they showed interest in (if they watched the price Reel fully, show the EMI breakdown)
- Behind-the-scenes content (builds familiarity with the store and people)
- Specific Bangalore references (creates "that's my city" connection)

**Example sequence:**
```
After price Reel engagement: "Rs. 999/month EMI. Here's exactly how it works."
After lifestyle Reel engagement: "3 Bangalore cycling routes that beat ORR traffic"
```

### Stage 3: RE-ACTIVATE (They engaged earlier but went quiet for 1-2 weeks)
**Goal:** Bring them back into the consideration cycle
**What works:**
- NEW information (not recycled content — something they haven't seen)
- Social proof with recency: "This week's test rides at BCH" / "3 more Bangalore cyclists switched this week"
- Seasonal/event hooks that create new urgency: "Petrol just hit Rs. [X]" or "Diwali offer live"

**Example sequence:**
```
"We sold 12 e-cycles this week. Here's what convinced them."
"New model just arrived. First look inside BCH."
```

### Stage 4: RE-CONVERT (They're interested but haven't taken action)
**Goal:** Provide the final push
**What works:**
- Direct CTA with extremely low friction: "DM 'READY' — we'll handle everything"
- Urgency through authentic scarcity: "Last 4 at this price"
- Risk reversal: "Test ride is free. If you don't love it, we didn't expect you to."
- Peer pressure: "37 people from [their area] bought from BCH this year"

```
  Post 1: Customer testimonial / delivery moment
  Post 2: Store activity / behind-the-scenes
  Post 3: Expert/authority content (25 years in business, 8 mechanics, etc.)
  PURPOSE: Build trust for considerers + provide co-decision ammunition

WEEK 4: CONVERSION + URGENCY
  Post 1: Conversion magnet (test ride CTA)
  Post 2: Scarcity/stock update
  Post 3: Seasonal/event trigger (or milestone content)
  PURPOSE: Convert ready buyers + create urgency for on-the-fence viewers
```

### Content Recycling Rules (for Retargeting Efficiency)

**Recycle every 6-8 weeks:**
Instagram's algorithm shows content to different users over time. A Reel that went to Audience A in January can be re-posted (with minor edits) and will largely reach Audience B in March.

**What to recycle:**
- Evergreen objection-killing content (safety, battery, price) — the objections don't change
- Cost comparison content (update the numbers for current petrol prices)
- Best-performing customer testimonials

**What NOT to recycle:**
- Seasonal content (Diwali content in February looks tone-deaf)
- Stock/scarcity content (outdated numbers break trust)
- Any content with specific dates or time-limited offers

**How to recycle:**
- Change the hook (same body, new first 3 seconds)
- Change the text overlays (fresh visual treatment)
- Add new information (update stats, add a new data point)
- Change the music/audio (algorithm treats it as new content)

# 5. CONTENT-TO-COMMERCE MEASUREMENT

## 5A. The Attribution Problem for Local Retail

### Why Measurement Is Hard for "Content to Store Visit to Sale"

**The measurement gap:**
```
MEASURABLE:                    UNMEASURABLE (without systems):
Reel views ✓                   Who watched AND came to store?
DM conversations ✓             Who saw content, didn't DM, but visited?
Profile visits ✓               How many Reels did they watch before visiting?
Website clicks ✓               Which specific Reel drove the visit?
Followers gained ✓             Did they show content to family? Did that matter?
Saves, shares ✓                What was the total content influence on the sale?
```

**The brutal truth:** For local retail without sophisticated tracking, you will NEVER have perfect attribution. But you can build a measurement system that gives you 70-80% visibility — which is enough to make good decisions.

---

## 5B. The Measurement Framework — 4 Layers

### Layer 1: Platform Metrics (What Instagram Tells You)
**Available from Instagram Insights (free):**

| Metric | What It Measures | BCH Benchmark Target |
|---|---|---|
| Reach | Unique accounts that saw your content | 50K-100K/month |
| Impressions | Total times content was displayed | 150K-300K/month |
| Engagement Rate | (Likes + Comments + Saves + Shares) / Reach | 5-8% (above 3% is good) |
| Save Rate | Saves / Reach | 2-4% (high-intent signal) |
| Share Rate | Shares / Reach | 1-3% (co-decision signal) |
| Profile Visits | People who visited your profile after content | 5K-15K/month |
| Link/Direction Taps | People who tapped "Get Directions" or link | Track weekly |
| DM Volume | Total DMs received | 50-100/week target |
| Follower Growth Rate | New followers / total followers per week | 2-5% weekly |
| Non-Follower Reach % | % of reach from non-followers | 50-80% (Reels) |

**Most important metric for BCH:** **Save Rate + Share Rate combined.** These are the highest-intent signals. A save = "I want to come back to this." A share = "I'm showing this to my co-decision-maker."

### Layer 2: DM Funnel Metrics (What Your Conversations Tell You)

| Metric | What It Measures | How to Track |
|---|---|---|
| DM-to-Qualification Rate | % of DMs where you identify a real lead | Manual count weekly |
| Qualification-to-Visit Rate | % of qualified leads who visit the store | Ask at store: "Did you DM us?" |
| Visit-to-Sale Rate | % of DM-sourced visits that buy | Track in billing/CRM |
| Average DM-to-Visit Time | Days between first DM and store visit | Manual tracking |
| Keyword CTA Performance | Which keywords drive most/best DMs | Track by keyword |
| Response Time | Average time to first reply | Manual or ManyChat data |
| Follow-Up Recovery Rate | % of non-responsive leads recovered | Track by follow-up |

**DM Attribution Tracking Template (manual):**
```
Weekly DM Log:
| Date | Name/Handle | Keyword Used | Commute | Budget | Model Recommended | Visited? | Bought? | Revenue |
```

### Layer 3: Store-Level Attribution (What Your Staff Can Track)

**The "How Did You Hear About Us?" System**
This is the MOST IMPORTANT measurement for BCH. Every single customer who walks in should be asked:

**Question 1:** "How did you find out about BCH?"
- Options: Instagram / YouTube / WhatsApp / Google / Friend/family referral / Saw the store / Other

**Question 2 (if Instagram):** "Do you remember which type of content you saw?"
- Options: Video about price/EMI / Customer review / Store walkthrough / Comparison video / Other

**Question 3 (if Instagram):** "Did you DM us before visiting?"
- Options: Yes / No

**Question 4:** "Did someone else help you decide to visit?"
- Options: Yes — spouse/partner / Yes — parent / Yes — friend / No — decided alone

**Question 5:** "How long ago did you first see our content?"
- Options: Today / This week / 1-2 weeks ago / 2-4 weeks ago / 1+ month ago

### Store Attribution Tracking Template
```
Daily Store Visit Log:
| Date | Customer Name | Source | Content Type | DMed First? | Co-Decider? | Days Since Discovery | Model | Sale Amount | EMI? |
```

**This is non-negotiable.** Without this data, you are flying blind. Even imperfect data from this system is 100x more useful than zero store-level attribution.

### Layer 4: Revenue Attribution (What Your Sales Data Tells You)

**The Content-to-Revenue Formula:**
```
Monthly Content Revenue =
  (DM-Sourced Sales) + (Content-Attributed Walk-Ins * Average Sale Value)

Where:
  DM-Sourced Sales = sales where customer DMed before visiting
  Content-Attributed Walk-Ins = walk-ins who said "Instagram" in store survey

Content ROI = Monthly Content Revenue / Monthly Content Cost
  Content Cost = (Hours spent creating content * hourly rate) + (Equipment/software costs)
```

**BCH Simplified Calculation Example:**
```
Suppose in one month:
  - 200 DMs received
  - 30 DM conversations → store visit (15% DM-to-visit)
  - 25 of those bought (83% visit-to-sale, matching BCH's 88%)
  - Average sale: Rs. 45,000
  - DM-sourced revenue: 25 x Rs. 45,000 = Rs. 11,25,000

  - 50 additional walk-ins said "Instagram" (no DM)
  - 42 of those bought (84%)
  - Walk-in sourced revenue: 42 x Rs. 45,000 = Rs. 18,90,000

  Total Content-Attributed Revenue: Rs. 30,15,000/month
  Content Cost: Rs. 50,000/month (staff time + equipment)
  Content ROI: 60:1
```

## 5D. Online-to-Offline (O2O) Measurement Techniques

### Technique 1: The "Mention This Reel" Mechanic
**How it works:** Include a specific offer or benefit in the Reel that can only be redeemed by mentioning it at the store.
- "Show this Reel at BCH Jayanagar → free helmet upgrade"
- "Say 'I saw the Doodle video' for a free first service"

**Measurement:** Count how many people mention the specific Reel at the store.
**Accuracy:** High (direct attribution) but captures only a fraction of total influence (many won't mention it).

### Technique 2: The "DM Keyword" Attribution
**How it works:** Different Reels use different DM keywords.
- Price Reel: "DM 'PRICE'"
- Comparison Reel: "DM 'COMPARE'"
- Test Ride Reel: "DM 'RIDE'"

**Measurement:** Track which keywords generate the most DMs AND which keywords lead to the most store visits/sales.
**Accuracy:** High for DM-sourced leads. Does not capture people who watched but never DMed.

### Technique 3: The "Weekly Unique CTA" System
**How it works:** Each week, the CTA includes a unique element:
- Week 1: "DM 'JANUARY1'"
- Week 2: "DM 'JANUARY2'"
- Or: "Mention 'Saturday special'" / "Ask for the 'Reel deal'"

**Measurement:** Each unique CTA tells you exactly WHEN the content was seen and which week's content drove the action.
**Accuracy:** Medium. Captures DM-sourced and mention-based attribution only.

### Technique 4: The "Google Maps Direction Tap" Proxy
**How it works:** Track "Get Directions" taps from your Instagram profile weekly. Correlate with content posting schedule.
**Measurement:** Spike in direction taps after a Reel = that Reel drove store visit intent.
**Accuracy:** Low-medium (correlation, not causation). But useful as a directional signal.

### Technique 5: The "Post-Purchase Survey" (Most Important)
**How it works:** After every sale, the billing staff asks the 5 attribution questions (from Layer 3 above).
**Measurement:** Direct customer-reported attribution.
**Accuracy:** Medium-high. Customers may not remember exactly which Reel influenced them, but they can tell you the channel (Instagram), the timing (this week / last month), and whether a co-decision-maker was involved.

## 5E. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) — What to Actually Optimize

### Vanity Metrics (Track but Don't Optimize For)
- Follower count
- Total Reel views
- Total likes
- Comments (unless they're purchase-intent comments)

### Consideration Metrics (Track and Monitor)
- Save rate (high-intent signal)
- Share rate (co-decision signal)
- Profile visits from Reels
- "Get Directions" taps
- Average watch time (content quality signal)

### Conversion Metrics (OPTIMIZE FOR THESE)
| KPI | Formula | Target | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|---|
| **DM Conversion Rate** | DMs / Reel Reach | 1-3% | Measures content's ability to trigger action |
| **DM-to-Visit Rate** | Store Visits from DM / Total DMs | 15-25% | Measures DM conversation quality |
| **Content-Attributed Revenue** | Revenue from Instagram-sourced customers | Track trend | The bottom line |
| **Cost per Content-Attributed Customer** | Content cost / number of attributed customers | Rs. 500-1,500 | Efficiency of content investment |
| **Time to Conversion** | Average days from first content view to purchase | 7-21 days | Measures funnel speed |
| **Co-Decision Rate** | % of sales involving co-decision-maker | Track trend | Validates co-decision content investment |
| **Content-to-Store Ratio** | Store visits attributed to content / Total store visits | 40-60% | Measures content's overall contribution |

### The "North Star" Metric for BCH
**Content-Attributed Revenue per Reel = Total Monthly Content-Attributed Revenue / Reels Posted**

This single metric tells you: "For every Reel we post, how much revenue does it eventually generate?"

Target: Rs. 50,000-1,00,000 per Reel (at BCH's price point and conversion rate)

If this number is going up month-over-month, the content strategy is working. If it's flat or declining, something in the funnel is broken.

## 5F. Social Media Revenue Attribution Models

### Model 1: First-Touch Attribution
**How it works:** All revenue is attributed to the FIRST piece of content the customer saw.
**Best for:** Understanding which content brings NEW people into the funnel.
**Problem:** Ignores all the nurture touches that moved them to purchase.

### Model 2: Last-Touch Attribution
**How it works:** All revenue is attributed to the LAST piece of content before the store visit/DM.
**Best for:** Understanding which content triggers the final decision.
**Problem:** Ignores all the awareness/consideration content that warmed them up.

### Model 3: Linear Attribution
**How it works:** Revenue is split equally across all content touchpoints.
**Best for:** Valuing the entire content ecosystem.
**Problem:** Gives equal credit to a casual first view and a decisive conversion magnet.

### Model 4: Time-Decay Attribution
**How it works:** More recent touchpoints get more credit than older ones.
**Best for:** Reflecting reality — recent content influenced the decision more.
**Problem:** Still undervalues early awareness content.

### Recommended Model for BCH: "Simplified First + Last"
**How it works:**
- 40% credit to the FIRST content piece (what brought them in)
- 40% credit to the LAST content piece (what made them act)
- 20% credit split across all middle touches

**Why this works for BCH:**
- You can identify "first touch" through the store survey: "When did you first see our content?"
- You can identify "last touch" through: "What made you decide to visit today?" or the DM keyword
- You acknowledge the middle touches exist without needing to track every one

**Practical implementation:**
```
Customer: Rahul, bought Sherpa Z1 for Rs. 34,999
First touch: "Saw your petrol comparison Reel 3 weeks ago" (= CM-2 type)
Last touch: "DMed 'RIDE' after seeing the Saturday test ride Reel" (= Conversion Magnet)
Middle: Unknown number of Reels seen in between

Attribution:
  CM-2 (Commuter Math): Rs. 14,000 credit (40%)
  Conversion Magnet: Rs. 14,000 credit (40%)
  Other content: Rs. 7,000 credit (20%)
```

Over time, aggregate this data to see which content CATEGORIES drive the most first-touch and last-touch credit. This tells you what to make more of.
