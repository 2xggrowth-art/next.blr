## Level 1: Follow Magnet (PROVEN)

### The Formula

### Validated Mechanics (from NB-M1)
- **Explicit Gate:** "ONLY FOR FOLLOWERS" text at 0:00

## Level 2: Decision Magnet (NEXT TO BUILD)

### What Decision Magnets Do
Instead of gating behind a follow, you gate behind actions that move the viewer closer to buying:
- **Save** (viewer bookmarks for later = high purchase intent signal)
- **DM a keyword** (opens a conversation = warm lead)
- **Visit profile/link** (enters the sales funnel)

### The Psychology
At 10K followers, your content reaches people who already follow. These people know the brand exists. The bottleneck is no longer awareness â€” it's **decision**.

The buying decision for a Rs. 35,000-60,000 e-cycle has 5 barriers:
1. "Is it worth the money?" (value uncertainty)
2. "Will it work for MY commute?" (fit uncertainty)
3. "What if the battery dies / breaks?" (risk uncertainty)
4. "What will people think?" (social uncertainty)
5. "Where do I even start?" (action uncertainty)

**Decision magnets attack these barriers one at a time, inside entertaining content.**

### 5 Decision Magnet Formats

**DM-1: The Commuter Math Magnet (attacks barrier #1: value)**
- Content: Dramatically calculate the real cost comparison. Physical cash stacks. Receipts.
- "Petrol per month: Rs. 4,000. Auto/Uber: Rs. 6,000. E-cycle per month: Rs. 350 in electricity."
- Gate: "Save this. Next time your petrol bill hits, you'll need these numbers."
- CTA variant: "DM 'MATH' â€” we'll calculate YOUR specific commute savings."
- Why it works: Saves = algorithm boost + viewer revisits when they see their next petrol bill

**DM-2: The "Your Commute" Magnet (attacks barrier #2: fit)**
- Content: Map a real Bangalore commute on the e-cycle. Film it. Time it.
- "Koramangala to Whitefield. Car: 1h 40m. E-cycle: 55 minutes. Zero parking."
- Gate: "DM your route â€” we'll tell you if an e-cycle works for it."
- Why it works: Personalizes the product. DM = warm lead. Every DM = sales conversation starter.

**DM-3: The Objection Destroyer (attacks barrier #3: risk)**
- Content: Take the most common objection and destroy it on camera with proof.
- "Battery dies in 6 months? Here's a cycle we sold 18 months ago. [show battery meter: still at 85%]"
- Gate: "Comment your biggest doubt. We'll film answers to the top 5."
- Why it works: Comments = engagement boost + you learn real objections + content ideas forever

**DM-4: The Identity Flex (attacks barrier #4: social)**
- Content: Show real Bangalore riders (techies, startup founders, women commuters) riding e-cycles.
- "3 software engineers at [tech company] cycle to work. Here's why."
- Gate: "Tag someone who needs to see this" / "Share with your Bangalore group"
- Why it works: Normalizes e-cycling among TG. Shares = social proof. Tagging = warm referral.

**DM-5: The "One Step" Magnet (attacks barrier #5: action)**
- Content: Remove ALL friction from the next step. Make it dead simple.
- "Don't know which cycle fits you? DM 'QUIZ' â€” we'll ask you 3 questions and recommend one."
- Gate: "DM 'QUIZ'" or "DM 'TEST RIDE'"
- Why it works: The person doesn't need to research. One DM = they're in the funnel.

## Key Principles

**1. The magnet works because it's the exception, not the rule.**
If every video is a magnet, viewers learn to ignore the gate. Magnet content = surprise. Routine kills it.

**2. Regular content builds the BRAND. Conversion magnets drive the REVENUE.**
75% regular, 25% magnet (split across all 3 levels). Both are necessary.

**3. The store is the closer. Content is the opener.**
88% conversion rate at the store means your content's ONLY job is to get them through the door within 7 days.

**4. Proof > promises. Physical > verbal. Specific > general.**
Show the battery meter. Stack the cash. Film the real customer. Name the neighborhood. Every conversion magnet lives or dies on proof density.

**5. Every conversion magnet must answer: "Where do I go, when, and what do I ask for?"**
If the viewer finishes the Reel and doesn't know these 3 things, the magnet failed.
